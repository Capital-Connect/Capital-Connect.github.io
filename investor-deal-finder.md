# Deal Finder

> Search across thousands of businesses to discover the right investment opportunities. Access Deal Finder at [app.capitalconnect.africa/investor/deal-finder](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/investor/deal-finder)

## Overview

Deal Finder allows you to browse and filter businesses across the platform based on your investment preferences.

## What This Section Is
 
Deal Finder is the investor's proactive search tool. Where the Dashboard surfaces businesses the platform has matched to the investor's profile, Deal Finder lets the investor go looking — browsing and filtering across all businesses on the platform based on their own criteria. It is the difference between waiting for matches to come to you and actively searching the deal flow.
 
**Your framing:** *"Deal Finder is your proactive search — you set the filters and browse what's out there, rather than waiting for the matching engine to surface opportunities. It's most useful when your Dashboard matches are sparse, or when you want to explore outside your standard criteria."*
 
**When You route an investor to Deal Finder:**
- Dashboard shows zero or very few matched businesses
- Investor wants to explore a sector or geography outside their current profile criteria
- Investor is looking for a specific type of opportunity not surfacing in matched results
- Investor wants to compare businesses across criteria before updating their profile

## Using Filters

1. Click **Open Filter** to expand the filter panel
2. Enter your desired criteria
3. The platform will filter opportunities to match your preferences

| Filter         | Description |
|---|---|
| Sector         | Filter by industry or business sector |
| Country        | Filter by country of operation |
| Growth Stage   | Filter by business stage (e.g. Early Stage, Growth Stage) |
| Funds Needed   | Filter by investment amount being sought |
| Investment Structure | Filter by preferred investment type |

## How You Guide the Investor Using the Filters
 
Click **Open Filter** to expand the filter panel. Set criteria, then apply. The platform filters available businesses to match the selected preferences.
 
### The Five Filters
 
| Filter           | What It Does | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Sector**       | Filter by industry or business sector | "Select the sector you want to explore. If you're open across sectors, leave this blank and use other filters to narrow down." |
| **Country**      | Filter by country of business operation | "Select the country or region you're deploying into. If you cover multiple countries, run separate searches per country for a cleaner view." |
| **Growth Stage** | Filter by business stage — Idea, Validation, Early Stage, Growth, Mature | "Select the stage you fund. If you're flexible, start broad and narrow from there." |
| **Funds Needed** | Filter by the investment amount the business is seeking | "Set a range that overlaps with your ticket size. Too narrow and you'll miss good fits; too broad and you'll have too many to review." |
| **Investment Structure** | Filter by the type of investment the business is seeking — Equity, Debt, Grant, Convertible Note, Revenue Share | "Only select structures you actually offer. Connecting with a business that wants equity when you only do debt wastes both parties' time." |
 
**Your filter sequence recommendation:**
1. Set Sector first — the strongest narrowing filter
2. Add Country second — geography is the second biggest qualifier
3. Add Growth Stage third — stage alignment is a hard filter for most investors
4. Add Investment Structure fourth — eliminates structural mismatches
5. Add Funds Needed last — use a range, not a precise figure

## Interpreting Results
 
After applying filters, the platform returns a list of matching business cards. You guide the investor through reading results analytically — not as a list to scroll through, but as a pipeline to evaluate.
 
**Your results guidance:**
 
| Result                             | What You Read | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| Strong result set (10+ businesses) | Filters are well-calibrated | "You have a good set to work with. Start with businesses whose readiness scores are highest — they'll be the most investor-ready." |
| Sparse results (1–5 businesses)    | Criteria may be too narrow | "The filters are returning very few results. Let's broaden one parameter — usually Funds Needed range or Growth Stage is the easiest to flex." |
| Zero results                       | Filters are too narrow or criteria combination has no matches | "Nothing came back. Let's clear one filter at a time and rerun — starting with Funds Needed, then Growth Stage — to identify which criteria is the binding constraint." |
 
**Your no-results diagnostic — one filter removed at a time:**

1. Remove Funds Needed filter → rerun → still zero?
2. Remove Investment Structure filter → rerun → still zero?
3. Remove Growth Stage filter → rerun → still zero?
4. Remove Country filter → rerun → still zero?
5. Remove Sector filter → rerun
→ At the step where results appear, that removed filter was the binding constraint
 
**Your line when results appear after removing a filter:** *"Results appeared after removing the [filter] constraint — that was the binding filter. Consider whether you can flex that criteria, or whether it needs to stay firm for your mandate."*

## Viewing a Business
 
Click on any business card to view its full profile — readiness scores, financial details, documents, sector, stage, and funding ask.
 
**What You tell the investor to look for before requesting a connection:**
 
| Check                | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| Readiness Scores     | Eligibility and Preparedness Scores above 80% signal investor-ready businesses |
| Funding Ask          | Does it fall within the investor's actual ticket size? |
| Growth Stage | Is this the stage the investor funds? |
| Investment Structure | Does the business's preferred structure match what the investor offers? |
| Documents            | Are a Pitch Deck and Business Teaser uploaded? Incomplete document packs slow due diligence. |
| Sector & Geography   | Final confirmation that both match the investor's mandate |
 
**Your line before a connection request:** *"Before requesting a connection, check the four alignment points: stage, ticket size, investment structure, and documents. A connection request to a misaligned business creates noise for both sides."*
 
---
 
## Requesting a Connection
 
After viewing a business profile that meets the investor's criteria, the investor can express interest to initiate a connection.
 
**Your instruction:** *"If a business looks like a strong fit — readiness scores above 80%, documents uploaded, and mandate aligned — click to request a connection. That moves them into your Connection Requests pipeline on the Dashboard."*
 
**After a connection request is sent:**
- The business appears under Connection Requests on the Dashboard
- If the business accepts, they move to Connected Businesses
- The investor can then access the full document pack and begin due diligence

## Deal Finder vs Matched Businesses — When to Use Each
 
You help investors understand the difference so they use the right tool at the right time:
 
| Tool                               | What It Does                | Use It When |
|---|---|---|
| **Matched Businesses (Dashboard)** | Platform surfaces businesses based on the investor's saved profile criteria                     | Reviewing the platform's recommended pipeline; starting point for any session |
| **Deal Finder**                    | Investor actively searches with custom filters | Dashboard matches are sparse; exploring outside usual criteria; testing new parameters before updating the profile |
 
**Your rule:** Always check Matched Businesses on the Dashboard first. Use Deal Finder to supplement, not replace, the matched pipeline.

## Possible Issues

- **No results returned** — your filter criteria may be too narrow
- **Filter not applying** — ensure you click the apply or search button after setting criteria

## Possible Remedies

- Broaden your filter criteria to return more results
- Clear all filters and start fresh using the reset option
- If the issue persists, contact our office

## Possible Issues & Your Remedies
 
One diagnostic at a time.
 
| Issue                       | Cause                                  | Your Remedy |
|---|---|---|
| No results returned         | Filter criteria too narrow | "Remove one filter at a time — starting with Funds Needed — and rerun after each removal. The step where results appear tells you which filter is the binding constraint." |
| Filter not applying        | Apply or search button not clicked after setting criteria | "After setting your filters, make sure you click the apply or search button — filters don't update automatically until confirmed." |
| Results don't match expectations | Filters set but criteria misaligned with available businesses | "Clear all filters, start fresh, and rebuild them one at a time — Sector first, then Country, then Stage. This helps identify where the mismatch is." |
| Issue persists after all checks | Platform error | "If the issue continues after clearing and resetting filters, contact the Capital Connect Africa office." |

## Your Priority Order in Deal Finder
 
1. **Start with Matched Businesses on the Dashboard** — always check matched pipeline first
2. **Open Deal Finder if Dashboard is sparse** — or if exploring outside usual criteria
3. **Set filters in sequence** — Sector → Country → Stage → Structure → Funds Needed
4. **If zero results** — remove filters one at a time to identify the binding constraint
5. **Review business profiles before requesting connection** — check 4 alignment points first
6. **After connections** — track them on the Dashboard under Connection Requests
