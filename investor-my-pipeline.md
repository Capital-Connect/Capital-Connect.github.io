# My Pipeline

> Track and manage the businesses in your investment pipeline. Access My Pipeline at [app.capitalconnect.africa/investor/pipeline](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/investor/pipeline)

---

## Overview

My Pipeline gives you a view of all the deals you are managing, along with analytics on deal activity and progress.
## What This Section Is
 
My Pipeline is the investor's deal management tool. It sits downstream of matching and connection — once an investor has identified and connected with a business, My Pipeline is where they track that deal through their own internal stages from initial interest through to close. It is the investor's CRM layer on the platform.

---
**The relationship between pipeline tools:**

Deal Finder / Matched Businesses → identify opportunities
Connection Requests              → initiate contact
Connected Businesses (Dashboard) → active relationships
My Pipeline                      → active deal management and tracking
 
**Your framing:** *"My Pipeline is where you manage deals that are actively in progress — you define the stages, add deals with their value and contact, and move them through as conversations develop. Think of it as your deal tracker on the platform."*

## Steps

### Adding a New Deal

1. Click the **New Deal ( + )** button
2. Fill in the form:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Deal Name | The name of the deal |
| Deal Value | The monetary value of the deal |
| Contact | The associated business or contact person |

3. Click **Add Deal** to save

### Configuring Deal Stages

You can customise the stages that deals move through in your pipeline.

1. Click the **Settings** button on the pipeline screen
2. A pop-up will appear — click the **( + )** button
3. Enter the name of the new stage and save


## Pipeline Overview — Three Metrics
 
At the top of the page, three summary metrics give a snapshot of pipeline activity:
 
| Metric             | What It Shows         | What You Read |
|---|---|---|
| **Paid Deals**     | Analytics on deals that have been paid | Completed transactions — use this to track deployment against mandate |
| **Closed Deals**    Analytics on deals that have been closed | Deals that have reached a final outcome — closed or declined |
| **Deal Stages**    | Breakdown of where each deal sits in the pipeline | The distribution of active deals across stages — identifies where deals are concentrating or stalling |
 
**Your first action on this page:** Read the three metrics to get a quick picture of pipeline health before drilling into individual deals.
 
**Your pipeline health read:**
 
| Signal                                          | What You Read |
|---|---|
| Many deals in early stages, few progressing     | Deals are being initiated but not advancing — review what's stalling early-stage conversations  |
| No Paid or Closed deals despite active pipeline | Deals are in motion but not completing — may need Advisory Session or deal structuring support |
| Pipeline is empty | No deals have been added yet — either no connections have been made or deals haven't been logged. Route to Connected Businesses on the Dashboard first. |

## Adding a New Deal
 
Click the **New Deal (+)** button to add a deal to the pipeline.
 
You walk the investor through each field one at a time:
 
### Field 1 — Deal Name
The name used to identify this deal in the pipeline.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Give the deal a name that's instantly recognisable — the business name plus a short descriptor works well. For example: 'AgriCo — Series A' or 'FinTech Ltd — Seed Round.' You'll be scanning this list frequently, so make it easy to identify at a glance."*
 
---
 
### Field 2 — Deal Value
The monetary value of the deal.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Enter the investment amount being deployed into this deal — in the currency you're using. This feeds into your Paid Deals and Closed Deals analytics, so accuracy here matters for tracking your deployment against mandate."*
 
**Your check:** *"Does the deal value fall within your stated ticket size on your Investment Profile? If it's significantly outside your range, it may be worth updating your profile criteria to reflect your actual deployment."*
 
---
 
### Field 3 — Contact
The associated business or contact person for this deal.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Link this deal to the business or contact person it relates to. This connects the deal to your activity history and makes it easier to track communications and next steps against the right business."*
 
---
 
### Saving the Deal
Click **Add Deal** to save.
 
**Lara's instruction:** *"Once all three fields are filled, click Add Deal. The deal will appear in your pipeline under whatever stage you've configured as the starting point. If it doesn't appear immediately, refresh the page."*

## Configuring Deal Stages
 
Investors can customise the stages deals move through — building a pipeline that reflects their actual investment process.
 
**Default stages are not prescribed by the platform** — investors define their own. Lara can suggest a standard deal stage framework if the investor hasn't set one up:
 
**Your suggested stage framework:**
 
1. Initial Review     — business identified, first look at profile and documents
2. First Meeting      — introductory call or meeting scheduled or completed
3. Due Diligence      — active review of financials, legal, team, and operations
4. Term Sheet         — term sheet issued or under negotiation
5. Closing            — documentation and funds transfer in progress
6. Closed / Won       — deal completed, investment deployed
7. Declined           — deal passed on at any stage
 

**Your framing:** You can build your stages to match your actual process. The ones I've suggested above follow a standard institutional deal flow — Initial Review through to Closing. If your process is different, name the stages to match how you actually work.
 
---
 
### How to Add a Stage
 
1. Click the **Settings** button on the pipeline screen
2. A pop-up appears — click the **(+)** button
3. Enter the name of the new stage
4. Save
**Your instruction:** *"Click Settings, then the + button in the pop-up. Type the stage name and save. If the new stage doesn't appear immediately after saving, refresh the page."*

## Moving Deals Through Stages
 
As conversations with a business develop, the investor moves the deal through their configured stages.
 
**Your guidance on deal progression:**
 
| Stage Transition               | What It Signals | Your Note |
|---|---|---|
| Initial Review → First Meeting | Enough interest to engage directly | "Before moving to First Meeting, confirm the business has a Pitch Deck and Financial Model uploaded to My Documents on their profile." |
| First Meeting → Due Diligence  | Sufficient preliminary interest to investigate further | "Moving to Due Diligence means requesting and reviewing full financial statements, legal documents, and the business valuation report if available." |
| Due Diligence → Term Sheet     | Investment thesis confirmed | "At this stage, a transaction advisor on either side is strongly recommended. Has the business indicated they have one?" |
| Term Sheet → Closing           | Terms agreed | "Closing involves legal documentation and funds transfer. Ensure all conditions precedent are cleared before moving here." |
| Any Stage → Declined           | Deal passed on | "Log the reason for declining — even if just internally — so you can identify patterns in what's not working in your pipeline." |
