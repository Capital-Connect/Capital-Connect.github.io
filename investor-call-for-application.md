# Call for Applications

> Publish calls for applications to source businesses that meet your specific funding criteria. Access Call for Applications at [app.capitalconnect.africa/investor/call-for-applications](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/investor/call-for-applications)

---

## Overview

Investors can create and manage calls for applications to invite businesses that match specific criteria to apply for funding.
## What This Section Is
 
Call for Applications is the investor's outbound sourcing tool. Instead of waiting for the matching engine to surface businesses, the investor publishes a structured funding call — specifying exactly what they are looking for — and businesses that meet the criteria apply directly. It is the investor-side equivalent of a targeted open call for deal flow.
 
**Your framing:** *"Call for Applications lets you go to the market with a specific ask — you define the criteria, the sectors, the geographies, the focus areas, and the questions you want answered. Businesses that fit apply directly to you. It's proactive deal sourcing on your terms."*

## Steps

### Step 1: Create a New Application

1. Click **Call for Applications** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Create Application** on the right-hand side
3. A form will appear — fill in the basic details of your call:

| Field              | Description |
|---|---|
| Title              | The name of your call for applications |
| Deadline           | The closing date for applications |
| Description        | An overview of what you are looking for |
| Focus Areas        | The ESG or thematic focus areas relevant to the call |
| Sectors            | The industries you want applicants to operate in |
| Target Countries   | The countries you are targeting for this call |
| Application Stages | The stages applicants will move through during review |

4. Click **Save** — your call will move to **Draft** status

---
**When You recommend using Call for Applications:**
- The investor has a specific mandate or thesis they want to fill with targeted applications
- Dashboard matches are not surfacing the right businesses and a broader sourcing approach is needed
- The investor wants to run a formal, structured programme — an accelerator, a grant round, or a sector-specific funding call
- The investor wants to collect standardised information from all applicants through a custom questionnaire
---
 
## The Three-Step Creation Process
 
You guide the investor through all three steps in order. A call is not live until it has been published from Draft status — completing Step 1 alone does not make it visible to businesses.
 
Step 1: Create the call → saved as Draft
Step 2: Configure questions → custom application questions
Step 3: Add required documents → specify what businesses must upload
→ Publish to make visible to businesses

## Step 1 — Create a New Application
 
### How to Access
 
1. Click **Call for Applications** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Create Application** on the right-hand side
3. Fill in the call details form
### The Seven Fields
 
You walk the investor through each field one at a time:
 
**Field 1 — Title**
The name of the call — visible to businesses browsing open calls on the platform.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Give the call a clear, specific title that tells businesses immediately what this is for. 'East Africa Agribusiness Seed Fund — Q3 2025' is more actionable than 'Investment Opportunity.' Businesses will use the title to decide whether to read further."*
 
---
 
**Field 2 — Deadline**
The closing date for applications.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Set a deadline that gives businesses enough time to prepare a strong application — typically 3–6 weeks from publication. Too short and you'll get rushed, incomplete submissions. Too long and the call loses urgency. What's your target close date?"*
 
**Your reminder:** *"Once the deadline passes, new applications can no longer be submitted. Make sure you're ready to review by the deadline — the review process starts immediately after it closes."*
 
---
 
**Field 3 — Description**
An overview of the call — what the investor is looking for, what the funding covers, and what successful applicants can expect.
 
**Your guidance:** *"The description is your call-to-action for the right businesses. Cover three things: what you're funding (sector, stage, geography), what you're offering (ticket size, investment structure, any non-financial support), and what you're looking for in applicants (impact focus, team quality, traction). Businesses use this to self-select — a clear description means better-quality applicants."*
 
---
 
**Field 4 — Focus Areas**
The ESG or thematic focus areas relevant to the call.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Select the impact or thematic areas this call targets — Climate, Gender Inclusion, Financial Access, Job Creation, and so on. This filters for mission-aligned businesses and signals your investment thesis to the market. If the call is purely financial with no ESG focus, leave this broad or unspecified."*
 
---
 
**Field 5 — Sectors**
The industries eligible to apply.
 
**Your guidance:** *"List only the sectors you will genuinely consider — vague sector coverage leads to off-mandate applications that waste everyone's time. If you're sector-agnostic, say so in the description rather than listing every sector. What sectors does this call target?"*
 
---
 
**Field 6 — Target Countries**
The countries whose businesses are eligible to apply.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Specify the countries you're deploying into for this call. Businesses from outside your target countries will apply if you leave this open — and reviewing ineligible applications is a time cost. Be specific."*
 
---
 
**Field 7 — Application Stages**
The stages applicants will move through during the review process.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Define the stages your review process will follow. A standard set looks like: Submitted → Under Review → Shortlisted → Due Diligence → Final Decision. You can customise these to match your actual process — but define them before publishing so applicants know what to expect."*
 
**Your suggested application stage framework:**
1. Submitted        — application received, awaiting review
2. Under Review     — active assessment by the investment team
3. Shortlisted      — selected for further consideration
4. Due Diligence    — deep review of financials and documents
5. Final Decision   — accepted or declined

---

Click **Save** — the call moves to **Draft** status.
 
**Your instruction:** *"After saving, your call is in Draft — it is not yet visible to businesses. You still need to add your questions and required documents in Steps 2 and 3 before publishing. Do not skip those steps."*

## Step 2 — Configure Questions
 
On the draft screen, navigate to the **Questions** section to add custom questions for applicants to answer.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Custom questions are how you collect the specific information you need to evaluate applicants — beyond what's already in their platform profile. Think about what you genuinely need to make an investment decision and build your questions around that."*
 
**Your suggested question categories:**
 
| Category        | Example Questions |
|---|---|
| Business model  | "Describe your primary revenue stream and how it generates income." |
| Traction        | "What is your current monthly revenue / number of customers / growth rate?" |
| Impact          | "What specific social or environmental problem does your business address, and how do you measure your impact?" |
| Team            | "Describe your founding team's relevant experience." |
| Use of funds | "How will you deploy the investment, broken down by category and percentage?" |
| Differentiation | "What makes your business competitively distinct from existing solutions?" |
| Stage readiness | "What is your current growth stage and what milestones will this funding help you reach?" |
 
**Your question design rule:** *"Ask only questions whose answers will genuinely affect your investment decision. Every question that doesn't filter or inform is friction for the applicant — and lower-quality responses. Five sharp questions beat fifteen generic ones."*
 
---
 ### Step 3: Add Required Documents

Specify the documents applicants must upload as part of their submission.

1. On the draft screen, navigate to the **Documents** section
2. Add the required document types applicants must provide

---
## Step 3 — Add Required Documents
 
On the draft screen, navigate to the **Documents** section to specify what businesses must upload with their application.
 
**Your guidance:** *"Required documents ensure every applicant submits the same baseline materials — so you're comparing applications on an equal footing. Specify only what you actually need at the application stage. Heavy document requirements upfront discourage strong but early-stage applicants."*
 
**Your recommended documents by call type:**
 
| Call Type                 | Recommended Documents |
| Seed / Early Stage        | Pitch Deck, Executive Summary or Business Teaser |
| Growth Stage              | Pitch Deck, Financial Model (3-year projections), Executive Summary |
| Institutional / DFI-style | Pitch Deck, Financial Model, Audited Financials, Business Plan |
| Impact / Grant Programme  | Pitch Deck, Impact Framework or Theory of Change, Budget Proposal |
 
**Your rule:** *"Do not require audited financials at the application stage for early-stage calls — most Idea and Validation stage businesses won't have them. Match your document requirements to the stage of business you're targeting."*
 
---
 
## Publishing the Call
 
After completing all three steps, the call must be published to become visible to businesses.
 
**Your pre-publish checklist:**

✅ Title is clear and specific
✅ Deadline gives businesses enough time (3–6 weeks recommended)
✅ Description covers: what you're funding, what you're offering, what you're looking for
✅ Sectors and countries are accurately specified
✅ Application stages are defined
✅ Questions are focused and decision-relevant
✅ Required documents match the stage of business being targeted
→ Publish

 
**Your instruction:** *"Before publishing, go through the pre-publish checklist. Once it's live, businesses can start applying immediately — changes to core fields after publication may confuse applicants who have already started their submissions."*
 
---
 
## Managing Applications After Publication
 
Once the call is live and applications begin arriving, the investor reviews and moves applicants through the configured stages.
 
**Your guidance on application review:**
- Check applications as they arrive — do not wait until the deadline to begin reviewing
- Use the configured stages to signal progress to applicants — move them through promptly
- Shortlist strong applicants early — this allows time for due diligence before the deadline closes
**Your guidance when reviewing an application:**
*"For each application, check three things first: (1) Does the business meet the basic criteria — sector, country, stage? (2) Is the application complete — all questions answered, all documents uploaded? (3) Does the business's readiness score on the platform support the claims in the application? Start with those three checks before reading the full submission."*

 ## Possible Issues

- **Application not saving** — ensure all required fields are filled in before saving
- **Call not visible to businesses** — the call may still be in Draft status

## Possible Remedies

- Review all required fields and ensure they are completed before saving
- Publish the call from the Draft screen to make it visible to businesses
- If the issue persists, contact our office

---
## Possible Issues & Your Remedies
 
One diagnostic at a time.
 
| Issue                           | Cause             | Your Remedy |
|---|---|---|
| Application not saving          | Required fields not completed | "Check that all required fields in Step 1 are filled — Title, Deadline, Description, Sectors, and Target Countries at minimum. The form won't save with empty required fields." |
| Call not visible to businesses  | Still in Draft status | "The call is in Draft — it's not live yet. Complete your questions and required documents in Steps 2 and 3, then publish to make it visible to businesses." |
| No applications received        | Call recently published or criteria too narrow | "If the call is newly published, give it time. If applications are slow after a week, check whether your Sectors and Target Countries are too narrow — or whether the description clearly communicates what you're looking for." |
| Issue persists after all checks | Platform error | "If the issue continues after verifying all fields, contact the Capital Connect Africa office." |
