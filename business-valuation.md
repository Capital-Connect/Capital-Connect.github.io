## What This Is
 
The Business Valuation is a paid report benchmarked against 167,000+ global transactions, delivered in minutes. It gives the founder a credible, data-backed valuation figure to anchor investor conversations, term sheet discussions, and fundraising strategy.
 
**You recommend this when:**
- The founder is entering or preparing for active investor conversations
- An investor has asked "what is your valuation?"
- The founder needs a defensible number before negotiating equity or deal terms
- The founder wants to strengthen their overall investor package
**You always recommend  the free AI Profile Analysis first.** The valuation is most useful once the profile and readiness gaps are addressed. Do not recommend it as a substitute for completing assessments.

### Step 3 — Risk Assessment
 
> Non-financial factors that provide qualitative context to the valuation. Each question is rated **1–5**.
> Accurate ratings ensure both quantitative and qualitative dimensions are captured in the final report.
 
**Your framing before this step:** *"This section rates your business on non-financial risk factors across seven categories. Each question is scored 1–5. Be honest — these ratings are used to contextualise your financial figures. Overstating strength here will produce a less accurate valuation."*
 
You guide the founder through each category in order. For each factor, You explain what the rating direction means so the founder scores correctly.
 
---
 
#### General
| Factor                        | Rating Direction      | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Size compared to competitors  | 1 = Large → 5 = Small | "Rate your size relative to competitors in your market — 1 if you're one of the larger players, 5 if you're much smaller." |
| Product/service positioning   | 1 = Premium → 5 = Commodity | "Rate your positioning — 1 if you're a premium, differentiated offering, 5 if your product competes mainly on price." |
 
#### Market
| Factor                             | Rating Direction           | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Size of addressable market         | 1 = Large → 5 = Small      | "How large is the total market you're operating in? 1 for a large, well-established market."     |
| Speed of market expansion          | 1 = High → 5 = Low         | "Is your market growing fast? 1 if it's expanding rapidly, 5 if growth is slow or flat." |
| Market share vs competitors        | 1 = High → 5 = Small       | "What share of the market do you hold? 1 if you're a market leader, 5 if you're early and small." |
| Degree of competition              | 1 = Low → 5 = High         | "How competitive is your market? 1 if you face little competition, 5 if it's highly contested." |
| Exposure to economic fluctuations  | 1 = Low → 5 = High         | "How sensitive is your business to economic cycles? 1 if you're recession-resistant, 5 if revenue drops sharply in downturns." |
| Exposure to political/legal changes | 1 = Low → 5 = High         | "How exposed are you to regulatory or political risk? 1 if the environment is stable, 5 if it's highly uncertain." |
 
#### Management
| Factor                        | Rating Direction      | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Quality of management team    | 1 = High → 5 = Low    | "Rate the depth and experience of your leadership team. 1 for a seasoned, complete team." |
| Reliance on key persons       | 1 = Low → 5 = High    | "How dependent is the business on one or two individuals? 1 if operations run independently of any single person, 5 if the business would struggle without a key person." |
 
#### Sales
| Factor                         | Rating Direction              | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Reliance on key customers      | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How concentrated is your revenue? 1 if no single customer makes up a large share, 5 if one or two customers drive most of your revenue." |
| Reliance on key distribution partners | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How dependent are you on specific distribution channels or partners?" |
| Customer lock-in and retention | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "How sticky are your customers? 1 if switching costs are high and retention is strong, 5 if customers leave easily." |
 
#### Purchasing
| Factor                       | Rating Direction  | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Risk of rising input costs   | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How exposed are you to cost increases in your inputs or raw materials?" |
| Reliance on key suppliers    | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How dependent are you on specific suppliers? 1 if you have multiple alternatives, 5 if losing one supplier would be critical." |
 
#### Product
| Factor                         | Rating Direction | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Quality of products/services   | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "Rate the quality of your offering. 1 for market-leading quality." |
| Level of innovation            | 1 = Highly Innovative → 5 = Not at all | "How innovative is your product or service? 1 if you're pioneering new technology or approaches." |
| IP rights protection           | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "How well-protected is your intellectual property? 1 if you have strong patents, trademarks, or trade secrets." |
| Risk of technological obsolescence | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How likely is your product to be displaced by new technology? 1 if the risk is minimal." |
 
#### Financial
| Factor                         | Rating Direction   | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Capital investment needs       | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How capital-intensive is your business? 1 if you can scale without heavy investment, 5 if growth requires significant capex." |
| Level of leverage              | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How much debt does the business carry relative to equity? 1 for a low-debt, conservatively financed business." |
| Exposure to FX fluctuations    | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How exposed is your business to foreign exchange risk? 1 if you operate entirely in local currency, 5 if significant revenue or costs are in foreign currency." |

## Steps

> Open the [New Business Valuation Form](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/valuations/information-gathering)
> Before starting, have your financial figures and business details ready for the best valuation outcome.

---

### Step 1: Contact Information
> Your contact details are used to notify you when the valuation report is ready.

1. **What is your name?** — Your full name
   > You can check *"Use my company information for this valuation"* to auto-fill your details from your account profile.
2. **Email address** — The email address where your report notification will be sent
3. **What is your phone number?** — Your phone number including country code
4. **Report Language** — The language your report will be generated in (default: *English*)

---

### Step 2: Company Information
> Financial data entered here is used to compute your valuation. Provide accurate figures for the most reliable result.

1. **What is the company's name?** — The name used throughout the valuation report. You may use a project name.
   > Changing the country or industry will affect valuation parameters such as tax rate, peer comparisons, and transaction benchmarks.
2. **In which country is the headquarters located?** — The primary country of operation
3. **In which industry does the company operate?** — The industry your business belongs to
4. **What was your company's total revenue in the last 12 months?** — Or projected annual revenue if pre-revenue. Enter amount and select currency.
5. **How have sales trends been over the past three years?** — Select the option that best describes your historical sales growth
   > Examples: *Sales have grown more than 10% annually, Sales have been flat, Sales have declined*
6. **How much money does your business currently have available?** — Cash, bank balances, mobile money, and short-term deposits convertible to cash within 3 months
7. **How do you expect sales to develop over the next three years?** — Your forward-looking sales growth expectation
8. **What is the total amount your business currently owes to others?** — Include bank loans, supplier credit, unpaid taxes, and all short and long-term borrowings
9. **What was your company's EBITDA in the last 12 months?** — Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, and Amortisation. Use projected figures if pre-revenue.
10. **What was your company's EBIT in the last 12 months?** — Earnings Before Interest and Tax. Use projected figures if pre-revenue.
11. **How do you expect your EBITDA margin to develop over the next 3 years?** — Your operating profit as a percentage of revenue going forward
12. **What was your company's EBIT margin over the last 12 months?** — Your earnings before interest and tax as a percentage of revenue

---

### Step 3: Risk Assessment
> Non-financial factors that provide qualitative context to your valuation. Each question is rated on a scale of 1–5.
> Accurate ratings ensure both quantitative and qualitative aspects are reflected in your final valuation.

Ratings are across seven categories:

**General**
- Size of the company compared to competitors *(Large → Small)*
- Product and service positioning compared to competitors *(Premium → Commodity)*

**Market**
- Size of the overall addressable market *(Large → Small)*
- Speed of market expansion *(High → Low)*
- Market share compared to competitors *(High → Small)*
- Degree of competition within the market *(Low → High)*
- Exposure to economic fluctuations *(Low → High)*
- Exposure to political and legal environment changes *(Low → High)*

**Management**
- Quality of the management team *(High → Low)*
- Reliance on individual key persons *(Low → High)*

**Sales**
- Reliance on key customers *(Low → High)*
- Reliance on key distribution partners *(Low → High)*
- Customer lock-in and willingness to stay *(High → Low)*

**Purchasing**
- Risk of rising input costs *(Low → High)*
- Reliance on key suppliers *(Low → High)*

**Product**
- Quality of products and/or services *(High → Low)*
- Level of innovation *(Highly Innovative → Not at all Innovative)*
- Intellectual property rights protection *(High → Low)*
- Risk of obsolescence due to technological advancements *(Low → High)*

**Financial**
- Capital investment needs *(Low → High)*
- Level of leverage employed *(Low → High)*
- Exposure to foreign exchange rate fluctuations *(Low → High)*

---

### Step 4: Additional Information
> Extra context about your business to improve the accuracy and relevance of the valuation report.

1. **Please briefly describe the company's activities** — A short overview of what your business does, the industry it operates in, and its size
   > Example: *"Our company is active in the fintech industry and has 25 employees."*
2. **Why are you interested in getting a valuation?** — Select your primary reason from the dropdown
   > Examples: *Fundraising, Merger & Acquisition, Internal planning, Loan application*
3. **Is there a publicly traded company similar to yours?** — Optional. Providing a comparable company improves benchmark accuracy.

- Wait a few minutes for the M-Pesa prompt — if it doesn't arrive, try again or use an alternative payment method
- If payment was deducted but the report is still showing *Payment Pending*, contact our office with your transaction reference
