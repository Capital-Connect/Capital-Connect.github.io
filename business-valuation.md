# Business Valuation

> Get a high-quality valuation report for your business, benchmarked against 167k+ transactions and delivered in minutes.
> This is a paid service priced at $99.99
> Access Business Valuation at [app.capitalconnect.africa/business/valuations](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/valuations)

---

## Requirements
- Contact details (name, email, phone number)
- Company name, country of headquarters, and industry
- Total revenue for the last 12 months (or projected if pre-revenue)
- Sales trends over the past 3 years and projections for the next 3 years
- Current cash and liquid assets available
- Total debt and outstanding obligations
- EBITDA and EBIT figures for the last 12 months (or projections if pre-revenue)
- EBITDA and EBIT margin expectations for the next 3 years
- Risk assessment ratings across general, market, management, sales, purchasing, product, and financial dimensions
- A brief description of the company's activities
- Reason for seeking a valuation
- A comparable publicly traded company (optional)
- Payment via Mobile Money or Card

## Steps

> Open the [New Business Valuation Form](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/valuations/information-gathering)
> Before starting, have your financial figures and business details ready for the best valuation outcome.

---

### Step 1: Contact Information
> Your contact details are used to notify you when the valuation report is ready.

1. **What is your name?** — Your full name
   > You can check *"Use my company information for this valuation"* to auto-fill your details from your account profile.
2. **Email address** — The email address where your report notification will be sent
3. **What is your phone number?** — Your phone number including country code
4. **Report Language** — The language your report will be generated in (default: *English*)

---

### Step 2: Company Information
> Financial data entered here is used to compute your valuation. Provide accurate figures for the most reliable result.

1. **What is the company's name?** — The name used throughout the valuation report. You may use a project name.
   > Changing the country or industry will affect valuation parameters such as tax rate, peer comparisons, and transaction benchmarks.
2. **In which country is the headquarters located?** — The primary country of operation
3. **In which industry does the company operate?** — The industry your business belongs to
4. **What was your company's total revenue in the last 12 months?** — Or projected annual revenue if pre-revenue. Enter amount and select currency.
5. **How have sales trends been over the past three years?** — Select the option that best describes your historical sales growth
   > Examples: *Sales have grown more than 10% annually, Sales have been flat, Sales have declined*
6. **How much money does your business currently have available?** — Cash, bank balances, mobile money, and short-term deposits convertible to cash within 3 months
7. **How do you expect sales to develop over the next three years?** — Your forward-looking sales growth expectation
8. **What is the total amount your business currently owes to others?** — Include bank loans, supplier credit, unpaid taxes, and all short and long-term borrowings
9. **What was your company's EBITDA in the last 12 months?** — Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, and Amortisation. Use projected figures if pre-revenue.
10. **What was your company's EBIT in the last 12 months?** — Earnings Before Interest and Tax. Use projected figures if pre-revenue.
11. **How do you expect your EBITDA margin to develop over the next 3 years?** — Your operating profit as a percentage of revenue going forward
12. **What was your company's EBIT margin over the last 12 months?** — Your earnings before interest and tax as a percentage of revenue

---

### Step 3: Risk Assessment
> Non-financial factors that provide qualitative context to your valuation. Each question is rated on a scale of 1–5.
> Accurate ratings ensure both quantitative and qualitative aspects are reflected in your final valuation.

Ratings are across seven categories:

**General**
- Size of the company compared to competitors *(Large → Small)*
- Product and service positioning compared to competitors *(Premium → Commodity)*

**Market**
- Size of the overall addressable market *(Large → Small)*
- Speed of market expansion *(High → Low)*
- Market share compared to competitors *(High → Small)*
- Degree of competition within the market *(Low → High)*
- Exposure to economic fluctuations *(Low → High)*
- Exposure to political and legal environment changes *(Low → High)*

**Management**
- Quality of the management team *(High → Low)*
- Reliance on individual key persons *(Low → High)*

**Sales**
- Reliance on key customers *(Low → High)*
- Reliance on key distribution partners *(Low → High)*
- Customer lock-in and willingness to stay *(High → Low)*

**Purchasing**
- Risk of rising input costs *(Low → High)*
- Reliance on key suppliers *(Low → High)*

**Product**
- Quality of products and/or services *(High → Low)*
- Level of innovation *(Highly Innovative → Not at all Innovative)*
- Intellectual property rights protection *(High → Low)*
- Risk of obsolescence due to technological advancements *(Low → High)*

**Financial**
- Capital investment needs *(Low → High)*
- Level of leverage employed *(Low → High)*
- Exposure to foreign exchange rate fluctuations *(Low → High)*

---

### Step 4: Additional Information
> Extra context about your business to improve the accuracy and relevance of the valuation report.

1. **Please briefly describe the company's activities** — A short overview of what your business does, the industry it operates in, and its size
   > Example: *"Our company is active in the fintech industry and has 25 employees."*
2. **Why are you interested in getting a valuation?** — Select your primary reason from the dropdown
   > Examples: *Fundraising, Merger & Acquisition, Internal planning, Loan application*
3. **Is there a publicly traded company similar to yours?** — Optional. Providing a comparable company improves benchmark accuracy.

## Possible Issues
- Report status shows *Payment Pending* — payment was not completed or was not confirmed
- Form does not proceed to the next step — not all required fields in the current step have been filled
- M-Pesa prompt not received — incorrect phone number entered or network delay

## Possible Remedies
- Verify all required fields are filled before clicking Next
- For M-Pesa, ensure the phone number is correct and in the format *+254 7XX XXX XXX*
- Wait a few minutes for the M-Pesa prompt — if it doesn't arrive, try again or use an alternative payment method
- If payment was deducted but the report is still showing *Payment Pending*, contact our office with your transaction reference
