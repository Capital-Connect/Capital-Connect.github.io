# Impact Assessment

> Measure the social, environmental, and governance impact your business creates.
> This is one of four investor readiness assessments we recommend completing to improve your visibility and attractiveness to investors — particularly impact-focused investors.
> Access the Impact Assessment at [https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/impact-assessment]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/impact-assessment

---

##  What This Assessment Is
> Priority: 4th of four investor readiness assessments
> Critical for: Impact-focused investors — DFIs, impact VCs, climate funds, gender-lens investors
 
The Impact Assessment measures and communicates the social, environmental, and governance (ESG) impact a business creates. It is the fourth and final investor readiness assessment. While it matters for all founders, it is especially critical for those targeting impact-focused investors — completing it well significantly improves matching with DFIs, impact VCs, and ESG-aligned funds.

**Your framing before starting:** *"This assessment asks about your ESG practices, SDG alignment, diversity initiatives, and governance structures. The stronger your answers, the more attractive you are to impact-focused investors — which make up a significant share of the active capital on this platform. Have your ESG data, DEI metrics, and SDG examples ready before we begin."*


## Requirements
- Diversity and inclusion practices within your workforce
- Social initiatives supporting employee well-being, community development, and human rights
- Environmental risk management and carbon footprint reduction efforts
- Alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- Governance structures ensuring transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior
- ESG stakeholder engagement practices
- Pay equity and equal opportunities policies
- Supply chain sustainability practices
- ESG and DEI metrics and data
- DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) initiative details
- SDG contribution data and examples

### Step 1: ESG Foundations
> Access at [step-one]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/impact-assessment/step-one

1. **What measures does your company take to ensure diversity and inclusion within its workforce, and how do you address unconscious bias in recruitment and promotion?**
   > Describe your D&I policies, hiring practices, and any training programs in place.
   > Examples: *Inclusive leadership development, Blind recruitment processes, D&I training programs*

2. **Can you provide examples of social initiatives your company has undertaken to support employee well-being, community development, and human rights?**
   > Highlight programs that go beyond the workplace into the wider community.
   > Examples: *Diversity and Inclusion Initiatives, Community health programs, Employee wellness stipends*

3. **How does your company assess and manage environmental risks, and what steps have you taken to reduce your carbon footprint and promote sustainability?**
   > Describe your environmental risk policies and any active sustainability measures.
   > Examples: *Setting emission reduction targets, Using renewable energy, Waste reduction programs, We utilise other methods*

4. **How does your company align its business strategy with specific Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) outlined by the United Nations, and what progress have you made towards achieving them?**
   > Reference specific SDGs relevant to your business and describe measurable progress.
   > Examples: *SDG integration into corporate strategy, SDG 8 Decent Work, SDG 13 Climate Action*

5. **What governance structures and policies are in place to ensure transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior within your organization?**
   > Describe your governance framework and oversight mechanisms.
   > Examples: *Board oversight committees, Ethics policy, Whistleblower protection, Independent audits*

**Q1 — Diversity & Inclusion Practices**
*"What measures does your company take to ensure diversity and inclusion within its workforce, and how do you address unconscious bias in recruitment and promotion?"*
 
| What You Look For                   | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| Specific D&I policies or programs   | Blind recruitment, structured interviews, diverse hiring panels |
| Training or development initiatives | D&I training programs, inclusive leadership development |
| Bias mitigation in promotion | Performance review calibration, diverse promotion committees |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Don't just say you value diversity — describe the specific practices. If you have blind recruitment or D&I training, name them. If you're early-stage and still building these, describe what you have and what you're working toward."*
 
---
 
**Q2 — Social Initiatives**
*"Can you provide examples of social initiatives your company has undertaken to support employee well-being, community development, and human rights?"*
 
| What You Look For           | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| Employee wellbeing programs | Wellness stipends, mental health support, flexible working |
| Community programs          | Community health, education, livelihood programs |
| Human rights alignment      | Fair wages, safe working conditions, no child/forced labor policies |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Give concrete examples — name the program, who it benefits, and roughly how many people it reaches. 'We run a community health clinic reaching 300 families' is far stronger than 'we support our community.'"*
 
---
 
**Q3 — Environmental Risk & Carbon Footprint**
*"How does your company assess and manage environmental risks, and what steps have you taken to reduce your carbon footprint and promote sustainability?"*
 
| What You Look For                      | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| Environmental risk assessment process  | Risk registers, environmental audits |
| Active reduction measures              | Renewable energy use, emission reduction targets, waste reduction |
| Measurable outcomes                    | Carbon reduced by X%, zero-waste operations, energy   efficiency improvements |
 
**You guidance:** *"If you use renewable energy or have set emission targets, say so and quantify it where you can. Even small steps — like switching to LED lighting or reducing paper use — are worth mentioning if they're part of a deliberate sustainability approach."*
 
---
 
**Q4 — SDG Alignment**
*"How does your company align its business strategy with specific Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) outlined by the United Nations, and what progress have you made towards achieving them?"*
 
| What You Look For                       | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| Specific SDGs named (not just "all 17") | SDG 8 Decent Work, SDG 13 Climate Action, SDG 5 Gender Equality |
| Integration into strategy               | SDGs reflected in mission, product design, or KPIs |
| Measurable progress                     | Jobs created, emissions reduced, financial access expanded |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Pick 2–3 SDGs that genuinely reflect your business model and describe specific progress. Naming all 17 SDGs signals you haven't thought about this seriously. Two well-evidenced goals beat a vague claim of alignment with all of them."*
 
---
 
**Q5 — Governance Structures**
*"What governance structures and policies are in place to ensure transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior within your organization?"*
 
| What You Look For                  | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| Board oversight mechanisms         | Board committees, independent directors, regular board meetings |
| Ethics and accountability policies | Ethics policy, code of conduct, conflict of interest policy |
| Whistleblower and audit mechanisms | Whistleblower protection, independent audits, financial controls |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Governance is often underdeveloped in early-stage businesses — be honest about where you are and describe the controls you do have. Investors respect honesty over overclaiming. If you have an advisory board rather than a formal board, say so."*

### Step 2: ESG Engagement & SDG Integration
> Access at [step-two]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/impact-assessment/step-two

1. **How do you engage with stakeholders, including investors, regarding ESG issues, and how do you integrate SDGs into your product development and innovation processes?**
   > Describe how ESG considerations are embedded in your business model and stakeholder communication.
   > Examples: *We engage with customers to understand their sustainability preferences, needs, and expectations*

2. **What efforts does your company make to address poverty, inequality, and social issues in line with the SDGs, and how do you ensure pay equity and equal opportunities for all employees?**
   > Highlight your pay equity policies and social impact programs.
   > Examples: *Social inclusion projects, Pay equity audits, Living wage commitments*

3. **How does your company ensure that its supply chain activities support the SDGs, particularly in terms of labor rights and environmental sustainability, and what mechanisms do you have in place to gather feedback from employees regarding diversity, inclusion, and equity issues?**
   > Describe your supply chain sustainability standards and internal feedback channels.
   > Examples: *Supplier engagement on SDG principles, Supplier code of conduct, Employee DEI surveys*

4. **Can you provide data or metrics demonstrating the impact of your company's ESG initiatives, SDG alignment efforts, and progress towards diversity, inclusion, and equity goals?**
   > Quantitative evidence strengthens your impact story with investors.
   > Examples: *Yes — carbon emissions reduced by X%, 40% female leadership, 500 community beneficiaries*

5. **Can you provide examples of how your company supports the professional development and advancement of underrepresented groups within the organization, and how do you create an inclusive workplace culture?**
   > Describe programs and initiatives that support underrepresented employees.
   > Examples: *We support Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) that cater to the interests and needs of underrepresented groups, Mentorship programs, Leadership pipelines for women*

|---|---|

| Poverty and inequality alignment | SDG 1 (No Poverty), SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities) contribution evidence |
 
**Your guidance:** *"If you've done a pay equity audit or committed to a living wage, say so explicitly. If your business model itself reduces poverty or inequality — e.g. you provide financial access to underserved communities — make that connection clearly."*

**Q3 — Supply Chain Sustainability & Employee Feedback**
*"How does your company ensure that its supply chain activities support the SDGs, particularly in terms of labor rights and environmental sustainability, and what mechanisms do you have in place to gather feedback from employees regarding diversity, inclusion, and equity issues?"*

| Employee DEI feedback channels | Annual DEI surveys, anonymous reporting, focus groups |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Two separate questions are embedded here — supply chain and employee feedback. Address both. If your supply chain is small or informal, describe what standards you apply even informally. For employee feedback, name the mechanism — survey, open-door policy, town halls."*

**Your guidance:** *"This is where numbers matter most. Even one or two concrete metrics — '40% of our leadership team are women' or 'we've created 120 jobs in rural communities' — are worth more than paragraphs of narrative. ### Step 3: DEI, SDG & ESG Metrics
> Access at [step-three]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/impact-assessment/step-three

1. **Provide an overview of your company's current DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) initiatives, including specific metrics or data that illustrate your progress and impact in this area.**
   > Include measurable outcomes where possible.
   > Examples: *% of leadership roles held by women, Number of DEI training hours delivered, Diversity hiring targets met*

2. **Explain how your company aligns its efforts with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and provide specific examples or data demonstrating your contributions towards these goals.**
   > Reference specific SDGs and provide evidence of impact.
   > Examples: *SDG 1 No Poverty — provided livelihoods to 200 smallholder farmers, SDG 13 Climate Action — reduced emissions by 15%*

3. **Detail your company's ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) initiatives, including metrics or data that reflect your progress and outcomes.**
   > Provide a consolidated summary of your ESG performance across all three dimensions.
   > Examples: *Environmental — zero-waste operations, Social — 1,000 community beneficiaries, Governance — independent board of directors*

## Step 3 — DEI, SDG & ESG Metrics
 
> URL: https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/impact-assessment/step-three
 
This is the metrics consolidation step. You push for specific data points across all three dimensions — DEI, SDG, and ESG — over narrative. This is the section that most directly determines the impact score investors see.
 
**Q1 — DEI Initiatives & Metrics**
*"Provide an overview of your company's current DEI initiatives, including specific metrics or data that illustrate your progress and impact in this area."*
 
| What You Look For                   | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| DEI initiatives named and described | ERGs, mentorship programs, blind recruitment, pay equity audits |
| Measurable DEI outcomes             | % women in leadership, diversity hiring targets met, DEI training hours |
| Progress framing                    | Where you started, where you are, where you're going |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Lead with data, then explain the initiatives behind it. '45% of our leadership team are women, up from 28% three years ago, driven by our leadership pipeline program' tells a complete story. Give the number, the direction, and the mechanism."*
 
---
**Q2 — SDG Contributions & Evidence**
*"Explain how your company aligns its efforts with the UN Sustainable Development Goals, and provide specific examples or data demonstrating your contributions towards these goals."*
 
| What You Look For                 | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|
| Specific SDGs named with evidence | SDG 1 — livelihoods to 200 farmers; SDG 13 — emissions reduced 15% |
| Measurable contributions          | Beneficiaries reached, jobs created, emissions cut, access expanded |
| Strategic alignment               | SDGs integrated into business model, not bolted on |
 
**Your guidance Example:** *"Name the SDG number and title, then give the evidence. Investors who care about SDG alignment will check whether your claims are credible — specific numbers and real examples are what make them believable."*
 
---
**Q3 — ESG Performance Summary**
*"Detail your company's ESG initiatives, including metrics or data that reflect your progress and outcomes."*
 
| Dimension            | What You Look For                   | Strong Answer Signals |
|---|---|---|
| **Environmental**     | Sustainability measures and outcomes | Zero-waste operations, renewable energy %, emissions reduced |
| **Social**            | Community and workforce impact | Community beneficiaries, jobs created, wellbeing programs |
| **Governance**        | Oversight and accountability structures | Independent board, audit completion, ethics policy in place |
 
**Your guidance Example:** *"Cover all three dimensions — Environmental, Social, and Governance — even briefly. Investors read ESG as a single package. A strong E and S with no G coverage leaves a gap. If governance is early-stage, describe the controls you have and what you're building toward."*
