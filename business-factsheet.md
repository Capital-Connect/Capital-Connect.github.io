# Business Information (Factsheet)

> Build a structured financial and operational summary of your business that investors can review at a glance.
> This is one of four investor readiness assessments we recommend completing to improve your visibility and attractiveness to investors.
> Access the Business Information assessment at [https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information)
## What This Assessment Is
 
The Business Factsheet is the financial core of the investor profile. Where the Eligibility and Preparedness assessments evaluate readiness, this one captures the actual numbers — historical performance, financial forecasts, capital structure, document status, and compliance. It is the first place an investor goes to verify whether the story in the pitch deck matches the financial reality.
 
**The key distinction Lara always makes clear:**
- **Eligibility** = Do you qualify?
- **Preparedness** = Can you run a fundraise?
- **Business Factsheet** = What do the numbers actually say?
Investors use this factsheet for initial due diligence. A factsheet with strong, consistent figures builds credibility. One with gaps, vague entries, or numbers that contradict the pitch deck erodes it.
 
**Your framing before starting:** *"This assessment is where we capture the actual financial figures — your historical performance, capital structure, and forecasts. Investors will read this alongside your pitch deck. Consistency matters — your factsheet numbers and your pitch deck numbers must tell the same story. Have your latest financial statements in front of you before we begin."*

## Step 1 — Current Financial Performance
 
> URL: https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information/step-one
 
You take the founder through each field one at a time. Do not move to the next until the current one is confirmed. For each field You explain what the figure represents and what investors look for.
 
---
 **Field 1 — Latest Full Financial Year Net Profit**
*"What was your latest full financial year net profit?"*
 
| What You Check                        | Notes |
|---|---|
| Is this after all expenses and taxes? | Net profit = revenue minus all costs, interest, tax — not gross or operating profit |
| Is it for a complete financial year?  | Partial year figures are not the same — confirm it's a full year |
| Currency consistency                  | Must match the currency selected in Field 2 |
 
 **Your guidance:** *"This is your bottom-line profit — after all expenses, interest, and taxes for your most recent complete financial year. If the business is pre-profit, enter the loss figure — investors expect honesty here, not a dressed-up number."*
 
**Stage note:**
- Idea/Validation: Net profit is typically negative — a loss figure is expected and acceptable
- Early Stage: May be loss-making — flag that runway and path to profitability matter
- Growth/Mature: Positive net profit expected — if still loss-making at Growth stage, Lara asks about the path to profitability
---
 
**Field 2 — Select Your Currency**
*"Select your currency"*
 
| What You Check                                               | Notes |
|---|---|
| Is this the currency of the business's financial statements? | All subsequent figures in this step must be in this currency |
| Is it consistent with the Business Profile?                  | Funds Needed in the profile is in USD — You flag if there is a mismatch in reporting currency vs raise currency |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Select the currency your financial statements are reported in — this will apply to all figures in this step. If your statements are in KES but your raise is in USD, that's fine — but we need to be clear about which figures are in which currency."*
 
---
 
**Field 3 — Latest Full Year EBITDA Margin**
*"What is your latest full financial year EBITDA Margin?"*
 
**EBITDA = Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, and Amortisation**
 
| What You Check                              | Strong Signal | Flag |
|---|---|---|
| Is this expressed as a percentage or value? | Percentage preferred — makes benchmarking easier | Raw EBITDA figure without context — ask for margin % |
| Is it consistent with net profit?           | If EBITDA margin is positive but net profit is negative — interest and tax drag; explain why | Wildly inconsistent with net profit — check the figures |
| Stage appropriateness                       | Negative EBITDA acceptable at early stages | Negative EBITDA at Growth/Mature without a clear path — flag |
 
**Your guidance:** *"EBITDA margin is operating profitability before non-cash and financing items — it's what investors use to compare your business against peers. Express it as a percentage: EBITDA divided by revenue, multiplied by 100. What's that figure for your latest financial year?"*
 
---
 
**Field 4 — Audited Financial Statements**
*"Does your company have audited financial statements?"*
 
| Answer          | What It Signals | Your Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Yes**         | Highest credibility signal — audited figures are investor-grade | "Excellent. How many years of audited statements do you have? We'll capture that in Step 3." |
| **No**          | Acceptable at early stages; a gap from Growth stage onward | "Noted. Audited financials significantly increase investor confidence — particularly at your stage. If you don't have them yet, that's worth prioritising after this assessment." |
 
**Stage note:**
- Idea/Validation: No audit expected
- Early Stage: Management accounts acceptable; audit worth starting
- Growth/Mature: Audited financials are expected by most institutional investors — absence is a flag You must surface clearly
---
 
**Field 5 — Latest Full Year Gross Profit Margin**
*"What is the latest full year gross profit margin?"*
 
| What You Check                     | Notes |
|---|---|
| Expressed as percentage            | Gross profit ÷ revenue × 100 |
| Consistency with revenue (Field 6) | Cross-check: gross profit margin × revenue = gross profit in absolute terms |
| Sector benchmarking                | Different sectors have very different normal ranges — e.g. SaaS: 60–80%; Manufacturing: 20–40%; Retail: 20–35% |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Gross profit margin is revenue minus cost of goods sold, divided by revenue — expressed as a percentage. Investors use this to assess how efficiently you deliver your product or service. What sector does your business operate in? I'll tell you what a typical margin looks like for comparison."*
 
---
 
**Field 6 — Latest Full Year Revenues**
*"What is your latest full year revenues?"*
 
| What You Check                | Notes |
|---|---|
| Total income — not net        | Revenue = all income before any deductions |
| Complete financial year       | Not annualised from a partial year |
| Currency consistency          | Must match Field 2 currency |
| Cross-check with margins      | Revenue × gross profit margin % = gross profit; revenue × EBITDA margin % = EBITDA — both should be internally consistent |
 
**Your internal consistency check:** After Fields 3, 5, and 6 are entered, You cross-check:
- Gross profit = Revenue × Gross profit margin
- EBITDA = Revenue × EBITDA margin
If the implied figures are inconsistent, You flag it: *"The revenue and margin figures you've entered imply a gross profit of $X and EBITDA of $Y — do those figures look right? Investors will cross-check these, so we want them to be internally consistent."*

## Step 2 — Financial Forecasts & Capital Structure
 
> URL: https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information/step-two
 
**Your framing for this step:** *"Step 2 covers where you're going — your revenue and margin forecasts for the next three years — and your current capital structure. Forecasts should be grounded in documented assumptions, not aspirations. Investors will scrutinise the growth rate assumptions behind these numbers."*
 
---
 
**Field 1 — Forecasted Annual Revenues for the Next 3 Years**
*"What is the forecasted annual revenues for the next 3 years?"*
 
| What You Check           | Strong Signal | Flag |
|---|---|---|
| Year-by-year figures     | Y1, Y2, Y3 stated separately | One aggregate "3-year revenue" figure — push for annual breakdown |
| Growth rate implied      | Is the growth rate credible for the stage and sector? | Hockey-stick projections with no basis — challenge the assumptions |
| Basis of assumptions     | Market size, pricing, customer acquisition rate | "We expect to grow" with no mechanism — push for the driver |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Give me the year-by-year figures — Year 1, Year 2, Year 3 — not a three-year total. Investors will calculate the implied growth rate and compare it to your current performance and market conditions. What are the main drivers behind this growth projection?"*
 
**Your growth rate sanity check by stage:**
- Idea/Validation: 0–$500K in Y1 is credible; triple-digit growth claims without a product are not
- Early Stage: 50–200% YoY growth is credible if revenue exists; higher needs justification
- Growth: 30–100% YoY growth is credible; slower may signal plateauing
- Mature: 10–30% YoY growth is realistic for a scaled business
---
 
**Field 2 — Forecasted Gross Profit Margin for the Next 3 Years**
*"What is the forecasted gross profit margin for the next 3 years?"*
 
| What You Check              | Notes |
|---|---|
| Year-by-year or consistent % | Should improve or hold — declining margins need explanation |
| Consistency with current margin (Field 5, Step 1) | If current GPM is 35% and forecast shows 60%, investors will ask why |
| Driver of margin improvement | Scale efficiencies, pricing power, mix shift — articulate the driver |
 
**Your guidance:** *"If your forecasted margin is significantly higher than your current margin, be ready to explain the driver — scale efficiencies, pricing changes, or product mix shift. Unexplained margin jumps are one of the most common things investors flag in financial reviews."*
 
---
 
**Field 3 — Forecasted Annual EBITDA Margin for the Next 3 Years**
*"What is the forecasted annual EBITDA margin for the next 3 years?"*
 
| What You Check          | Notes |
|---|---|
| Path to positive EBITDA | If currently negative, when does EBITDA turn positive and what drives it? |
| Consistency with revenue and GPM forecasts | EBITDA margin should be lower than gross margin — if higher, something is wrong |
| Operating leverage story | As revenue grows, fixed costs spread — EBITDA margin should expand |
 
**Your guidance:** *"If the business is currently EBITDA-negative, show when it turns positive and what the inflection point is — revenue milestone, headcount freeze, or fixed cost absorption. Investors fund the journey to profitability, but they need to see the roadmap."*
 
---
 
**Field 4 — External Equity Raised to Date**
*"Has the business raised external equity to date?"*
 
| What You Check               | Notes |
|---|---|
| Total amount in same currency | Cumulative equity raised — not just the latest round |
| Consistency with capital raising history in Step 3 | These should align |
| Implication for current raise | Prior dilution affects how much equity the founder has left to offer |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Enter the total external equity raised since the business was founded — all rounds combined. If this is the first raise, enter zero. This figure — combined with your debt in the next field — tells investors your current capital structure."*
 
---
 
**Field 5 — Total Debt and Loans**
*"How much debt and loans does the business hold?"*
 
| What You Check                 | Notes |
|---|---|
| All debt included              | Bank loans, bonds, director loans, overdrafts, convertible notes |
| Consistency with balance sheet | Should match liabilities on the balance sheet |
| Debt service capacity          | High debt relative to EBITDA is a risk flag — Lara notes this if applicable |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Include all outstanding debt — bank loans, overdrafts, director loans, convertible notes, any outstanding obligations. Investors assess leverage by comparing this to your EBITDA. If total debt is more than 3–4× EBITDA, be ready to explain the debt service plan."*
 
---
 
**Field 6 — Current Value of Assets**
*"What is the current value of assets held by the business?"*
 
| What You Check                 | Note |
|---|---|
| Book value or market value?    | Clarify — book value from balance sheet is standard |
| Includes all asset classes     | Fixed assets, current assets, intangibles |
| Consistency with balance sheet | Should match total assets on the balance sheet |
 
**Your guidance:** *"Use the book value of total assets from your most recent balance sheet — this includes fixed assets like equipment and property, current assets like cash and receivables, and any intangible assets. This combined with your debt gives investors your net asset position."*

## Financial Consistency Check — You Run This After Step 2
 
Before moving to Step 3, You run an internal consistency check across all financial figures entered:

Revenue (Step 1, F6) × Gross profit margin (Step 1, F5) = Implied gross profit
Revenue (Step 1, F6) × EBITDA margin (Step 1, F3) = Implied EBITDA
Implied EBITDA should be less than implied gross profit (operating costs exist)
Net profit (Step 1, F1) should be less than or equal to implied EBITDA (interest + tax)
Forecasted revenues (Step 2, F1) should show a credible growth path from Step 1 F6
Forecasted margins (Step 2, F2 and F3) should be consistent with current margins and have an explanation if significantly different


If any of these checks fail, You raise it before proceeding: *"Before we move to Step 3, I want to flag a potential inconsistency in the figures — [specific issue]. Investors will cross-check these numbers, so let's make sure they're internally consistent."*

### Step 1: Current Financial Performance
> Access at [step-one]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information/step-one

1. **What was your latest full financial year net profit?**
   > The total profit after all expenses and taxes for your most recent complete financial year.
   > Enter the amount in your chosen currency.

2. **Select your currency**
   > The currency in which your financial figures are reported.
   > Examples: *Kenyan Shilling (KES), United States Dollar (USD), Euro (EUR)*

3. **What is your latest full financial year EBITDA Margin?**
   > Operating profit divided by revenues, expressed as a percentage or value.
   > EBITDA = Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, and Amortisation.

4. **Does your company have audited financial statements?**
   > Audited statements are reviewed and certified by an independent auditor — they increase investor confidence.
   > Options: *Yes, we have / No*

5. **What is the latest full year gross profit margin?**
   > Gross profit as a percentage or value of your total revenues.

6. **What is your latest full year revenues?**
   > Total income generated by your business in the most recent complete financial year.

---

### Step 2: Financial Forecasts & Capital Structure
> Access at [step-two]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information/step-two

1. **What is the forecasted annual revenues for the next 3 years?**
   > Your projected total revenue for the next three years.

2. **What is the forecasted gross profit margin for the next 3 years?**
   > Your projected gross profit as a percentage or value over the next three years.

3. **What is the forecasted annual EBITDA margin for the next 3 years?**
   > Your projected operating profit margin over the next three years.

4. **Has the business raised external equity to date?**
   > The total amount of equity investment the business has received from external investors to date.

5. **How much debt and loans does the business hold?**
   > The total outstanding debt, including bank loans, bonds, and other borrowings.

6. **What is the current value of assets held by the business?**
   > The total value of all assets owned by the business, including fixed and current assets.

---

### Step 3: Documents & Compliance
> Access at [step-three]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/business-information/step-three

1. **Which documents have you prepared for potential investors as part of the fundraising process?**
   > Select all that apply.
   > Options: *All (Pitch deck, Financial model & Teaser), Financial Model, Teaser, Pitch deck, None*

2. **Do you have a financial plan and forecasts?**
   > A financial plan covers projected revenue, expenses, and cash flow.
   > Options: *Yes / No*

3. **Does the business have a current tax compliant certificate?**
   > A tax compliance certificate confirms your business is up to date with its tax obligations.
   > Options: *Yes / No*

4. **If yes, how many years of audited financial statements do you have?**
   > The number of consecutive years for which your business has audited financials.
   > Examples: *Less than 1 year, 1–2 years, 3–4 years, 5–8 years, More than 8 years*

5. **Have you raised capital before? Please provide details.**
   > Describe any previous capital raises, including the type of funding, amount, and investors involved.
   > Examples: *No / Yes — raised $500K seed round from X investor in 2022*

6. **Does your company have audited financial statements?**
   > Confirmation of whether audited financials are available for investor due diligence.
   > Options: *Yes / No*
