## What This Is — Investor Perspective
 
The Business Valuation report gives investors a data-backed, independent valuation of a business they are considering — benchmarked against 167,000+ global transactions including 4,500+ African deals and 53,000+ listed companies. It produces a credible, third-party figure that anchors deal negotiations, tests founder valuation claims, and strengthens due diligence.
 
**The key difference from the business side:** A founder uses this to generate their own valuation. An investor uses this to independently verify or establish a valuation for a business they are evaluating — before or during deal negotiations.
 
**Lara recommends this to an investor when:**
- A business in the pipeline is claiming a valuation that seems unsupported
- The investor wants an independent data point before issuing a term sheet
- Due diligence requires a third-party valuation to anchor equity negotiations
- The investor is comparing valuations across multiple businesses in a sector
- A business does not have a valuation report of their own
**Lara's framing:** *"The Business Valuation gives you an independent, data-backed figure for the business you're evaluating — benchmarked against real transactions in the same sector and market. It's most useful when you want to test the founder's claimed valuation or need a credible anchor before issuing a term sheet."*

### Step 2 — Company Information & Financial Data
 
This step captures the business's financial data. The investor enters figures for the business they are evaluating — not their own figures.
 
**Your framing for this step:** *"This step is about the business you're evaluating — not your own financials. Enter the business's figures from their uploaded financial statements and Factsheet. If you're not sure about a figure, cross-reference with their Income Statement and Balance Sheet on the platform before entering."*
 
| Field                 | What You Check |
|---|---|
| **Company name**      | The business being evaluated — not the investor's fund or firm |
| **Country of HQ**     | Primary operating country — affects tax rate and benchmark comparisons |
| **Industry**          | The business's sector — must be accurate for peer comparisons |
| **Total revenue — last 12 months** | From Income Statement or Business Factsheet. If pre-revenue, use projections |
| **Sales trends — past 3 years** | Historical growth direction — cross-reference with Factsheet |
| **Cash and liquid assets** | From Balance Sheet — Cash line |
| **Sales projections — next 3 years** | From Financial Model or Factsheet forecasts |
| **Total debt**        | From Balance Sheet — all borrowings including bank loans, director loans, convertible notes |

### Step 3 — Risk Assessment
 
Non-financial factors that provide qualitative context to the valuation. Each factor is rated **1–5** by the investor based on their own assessment of the business.
 
**Your framing:** *"This section is your independent qualitative assessment of the business — not the business's self-assessment. Rate each factor based on your due diligence findings. Be objective: overstating strengths will inflate the valuation and undermine the report's usefulness as a negotiation anchor."*
 
You walk the investor through each category in order, explaining the rating direction so they score correctly.
 
#### General
| Factor                       | Rating Direction                 | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Size compared to competitors | 1 = Large → 5 = Small | "How large is this business relative to its competitors in its specific market — not the industry overall?" |
| Product/service positioning  | 1 = Premium → 5 = Commodity | "Is this a premium, differentiated product or a commodity competing mainly on price?" |
 
#### Market
| Factor                      | Rating Direction      | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Size of addressable market  | 1 = Large → 5 = Small | "How large is the total market this business operates in?" |
| Speed of market expansion   | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "Is this market growing rapidly or is it flat/declining?" |
| Market share vs competitors | 1 = High → 5 = Small | "What share of the market does this business hold relative to competitors?" |
| Degree of competition | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How contested is this market — few dominant players or highly fragmented?" |
| Exposure to economic fluctuations | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How sensitive is this business's revenue to economic downturns?" |
| Exposure to political/legal changes | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How exposed is this business to regulatory or political risk in its operating environment?" |
 
#### Management
| Factor                      | Rating Direction            | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Quality of management team  | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "Based on your assessment — how experienced and complete is the leadership team?" |
| Reliance on key persons     | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "If the founder or a key person left, how severely would the business be affected?" |
 
#### Sales
| Factor                         | Rating Direction                      | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Reliance on key customers      | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How concentrated is the revenue base? Is there customer concentration risk?" |
| Reliance on key distribution partners | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How dependent is the business on specific distribution channels?" |
| Customer lock-in and retention | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "How sticky are the customers? High switching costs and strong retention = closer to 1." |
 
#### Purchasing
| Factor                     | Rating Direction   | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Risk of rising input costs | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How exposed is this business to input cost inflation?" |
| Reliance on key suppliers  | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How critical are specific suppliers? Would losing one be a serious operational risk?" |
 
#### Product
| Factor                       | Rating Direction   | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Quality of products/services | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "Based on your due diligence — how would you rate product/service quality?" |
| Level of innovation          | 1 = Highly Innovative → 5 = Not at all | "Is this business genuinely pioneering something, or operating in an established model?" |
| IP rights protection         | 1 = High → 5 = Low | "Does the business have meaningful IP protection — patents, trademarks, trade secrets?" |
| Risk of technological obsolescence | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How likely is this product to be disrupted or displaced by emerging technology?" |
 
#### Financial
| Factor                      | Rating Direction | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Capital investment needs    | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "Does this business require heavy ongoing capex to sustain growth?" |
| Level of leverage           | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "How leveraged is the business relative to its EBITDA? High leverage = closer to 5." |
| Exposure to FX fluctuations | 1 = Low → 5 = High | "If this business has cross-border revenue or costs, how exposed is it to exchange rate risk?" |

## Reading the Report — Investor's Lens
 
Once the report is ready, You help the investor interpret it in deal terms — not just as a document to file.
 
**What to look for:**
 
| Section                     | What You Focuses On |
|---|---|
| **Valuation range**         | Does it support, challenge, or contradict the founder's stated valuation? |
| **Benchmark comparisons**   | How does this business compare to the 167k+ transaction dataset? Above or below average for its sector and stage? |
| **Risk-adjusted figure**    | How much do the risk ratings pull the valuation down from the base figure? This tells the investor where the qualitative risks are most pronounced. |
| **Comparable company data** | If a comparable was entered, how does the benchmark compare? |
 
**Your valuation interpretation framework:**
 
| Scenario               | What You Read |
|---|---|
| Report valuation > Founder's claimed valuation | "The data supports a higher valuation than the founder is claiming — this is a positive signal. You may have more room on equity terms than initially assumed." |
| Report valuation ≈ Founder's claimed valuation | "The valuation is broadly consistent with the founder's figure. This strengthens the credibility of their ask." |
| Report valuation < Founder's claimed valuation | "The data doesn't support the founder's valuation. This is important for term sheet negotiations — use the report as the anchor, not the founder's figure." |
| Wide valuation range | "A wide range indicates significant uncertainty — often driven by the risk ratings. Review which risk categories drove the spread and factor those into your due diligence." |
