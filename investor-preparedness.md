# Investor Preparedness Assessment

> Assess how operationally and strategically ready your business is to receive and manage investment on Capital Connect Africa.
> This is one of four investor readiness assessments we recommend completing to improve your visibility and attractiveness to investors.

---

## Requirements
- Preferred capital raise structure
- Marketing materials prepared for investors
- Clarity on capital amount needed and its intended use
- A financial plan and forecasts
- A targeted list of suitable investors
- Understanding of the capital raising process
- Ability to defend your business valuation
- A strategy for addressing different investor types
- A transaction advisor (if available)
- Other financing options explored

---

## Steps

> Open the [Investor Preparedness Assessment Page]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/investor-preparedness
> After login, if the user has not completed their business profile, they will be automatically redirected to the profile setup page first.
> You can click **Save and Continue Later?** at any step to save your progress and return later.
> The onboarding progress bar at the top tracks completion across all four assessments: Investor Eligibility, Investor Preparedness, Business Information, and Impact Assessment.

### Step 1: Fundraising Preparedness
> Access at [step-one]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/investor-preparedness/step-one

1. **Do you have a preferred structure for the capital raise?**
   > The structure defines how you want to receive investment.
   > Examples: *Yes, we have a general feel of the capital raise structure / We have decided on Equity / No, we have not decided yet*

2. **Which marketing materials have you prepared for potential investors as part of the fundraising process?**
   > Marketing materials are documents you share with investors to make the case for your business.
   > Examples: *We have a teaser, a detailed pitch deck, and collated the relevant due diligence material / We have a pitch deck only / None yet*

3. **Do you know how much capital you are seeking to raise and what it will be used for?**
   > You should have a specific funding target and a clear breakdown of how the funds will be deployed.
   > Examples: *Yes, we are clear on what to raise, when, and the planned use of funds / We have a rough idea / Not yet*

4. **Do you have a financial plan and forecasts?**
   > Financial forecasts show investors where your business is headed.
   > This typically includes projected revenue, expenses, and cash flow for the next 3–5 years.
   > Examples: *Yes, we have a full 5-year financial model / We have basic projections / No, we do not have one yet*

---

### Step 2: Investor Readiness
> Access at [step-two]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/investor-preparedness/step-two

1. **Have you created a list of the most suitable investors for your company?**
   > A targeted investor list shows you understand who is likely to invest in your business.
   > Consider factors like sector focus, geography, ticket size, and investment stage.
   > Examples: *We have already contacted 5 investors / We have a list but have not reached out / No, we have not started*

2. **Do you have an in-depth understanding of the capital raising process?**
   > This covers knowing the stages of a fundraise — from initial outreach to term sheets, due diligence, and closing.
   > Examples: *Yes, we have done it before / We have a general understanding / I have no idea*

3. **Can you defend the expected valuation of your business?**
   > Investors will challenge your valuation. You should be able to back it up with comparable businesses, revenue multiples, or a discounted cash flow model.
   > Examples: *We know our valuation range, have benchmarks, and know how much equity to give / We have a rough valuation / We have not thought about it yet*

4. **How will you handle addressing different investors?**
   > Different investors have different priorities. Your approach to a venture capital fund will differ from an angel investor or a development finance institution.
   > Examples: *We know the structure of individual investor tickets, our timeline, and the type of process we want to run / We have a general approach / We have not planned this yet*

---

### Step 3: Additional Financing Considerations
> Access at [step-three]:https://app.capitalconnect.africa/onboarding/business/investor-preparedness/step-three

1. **Do you have a transaction advisor?**
   > A transaction advisor is a professional (lawyer, financial advisor, or investment banker) who guides you through the fundraising process.
   > Having one signals to investors that you are serious and well-prepared.
   > Examples: *Yes / No, but we are looking for one / No*

2. **What other financing options besides finding an investor have you explored?**
   > Investors appreciate founders who have considered all their options.
   > Examples: *We have some ideas of other cash sources / We have explored bank loans and grants / We have not explored alternatives yet*

## Possible Issues
- Form does not proceed to the next step — not all required questions in the current step have been answered
- Assessment page is not accessible — Business Profile has not been completed yet
- Progress not saving — use **Save and Continue Later?** to save before navigating away

## Possible Remedies
- Verify all questions in the current step are answered before clicking **Save & go next**
- Complete your Business Profile first to unlock the assessment
- If the issue persists after the above checks, contact our office

- **Go to Dashboard** — Return to the main dashboard
- **Take Investor Eligibility Assessment** — Complete or revisit the Eligibility Assessment
- **Take Impact Assessment** — Complete or revisit the Impact Assessment
