## Overview
## What This Section Is — Critical Framing
 
The Investment Scorecard is **not** a score of the investor. It is Capital Connect Africa's transparency tool that shows investors exactly how the platform scores the **businesses** (founders) they are evaluating. It reveals the scoring methodology behind the Eligibility Score and Preparedness Score that appear on every business profile.
 
**Your framing:** *"The Investment Scorecard shows you how Capital Connect Africa calculates the readiness scores you see on business profiles — the Eligibility Score and the Preparedness Score. When you see a business with a 78% Eligibility Score, this scorecard tells you exactly how that number was built: which questions were asked, how each answer was weighted, and what drove the final figure."*
 
**Why this matters for investors:**
- Removes the black box from readiness scores — investors see exactly what a score means
- Reveals specific gaps — a business scoring 65% on Preparedness may be weak on valuation defence or have no transaction advisor; the scorecard shows which
- Strengthens due diligence — investors can identify the exact gaps to probe in founder conversations
- Builds confidence in the assessment — investors know the scoring is rigorous and standardised, not arbitrary

---

###  Investor Eligibility Scorecard
 
Click the **Investor Eligibility** button to view the Eligibility Assessment questions and how each founder answer is weighted.
 
**What the Eligibility Score measures:** Whether a business has done the foundational preparation before approaching investors — capital raise structure, marketing materials, clarity on funding amount and use, financial forecasts, and fundraising process knowledge.
 
**Your framing for investors:** *"The Eligibility Score tells you whether a founder has cleared the basic bar before approaching you. A high score means they have a structure, a target, materials, and a plan. A low score tells you exactly where to probe — look at the scorecard to see which answers pulled their score down."*
 
**Score interpretation:**
 
| Score Range           | What It Means                 | Your Guidance for the Investor |
|---|---|---|
| 85–100%               | Founder is well-prepared on eligibility fundamentals | "Basic bar cleared. Shift your focus to Preparedness and the specifics of their financials and documents." |
| 70–84%                | Broadly eligible with some gaps | "Check the scorecard to identify which questions pulled the score below 85%. Those gaps are worth probing directly in your first conversation." |
| Below 70%            | Significant eligibility gaps | "Meaningful gaps exist. The scorecard will show you which answers were weak — use those as your opening due diligence questions before going further." |
 
---
 
### Investor Preparedness Scorecard
 
Click the **Investor Preparedness** button to view the Preparedness Assessment questions and how each founder answer is weighted.
 
**What the Preparedness Score measures:** Whether a business is operationally and strategically ready to receive and manage investment — investor list quality, process knowledge, valuation defence, investor type strategy, and transaction advisor status.
 
**Your framing for investors:** *"The Preparedness Score tells you whether a founder can run a professional fundraising process — not just whether they qualify, but whether they're ready to execute. A business with high Eligibility but low Preparedness is promising but not ready. The scorecard shows you exactly where they're weak."*

## How You Use the Scorecard in Investor Sessions
 
When an investor is reviewing a business, You use the scorecard to build a targeted due diligence agenda.
 
**Step 1 — Read both scores first**
*"What are this business's Eligibility and Preparedness Scores? Let's start there before looking at anything else."*
 
**Step 2 — Identify the weaker score**
*"The [Eligibility / Preparedness] Score is lower. Let's open that scorecard and see which specific answers drove it down."*
 
**Step 3 — Map score gaps to due diligence questions**
*"The scorecard shows their answer on [specific question] was rated low. That signals [specific gap]. Before progressing, ask the founder directly: [targeted question]. Their answer will tell you whether this is fixable or a structural issue."*
 
**Step 4 — Factor into pipeline prioritisation**
*"Both scores above 85% means this business is ready to engage — prioritise them. One score below 70% means more preparation is needed — assess the timeline before investing significant time in the conversation."*

## Scorecard-to-Due-Diligence Question Map
 
| Low-Weighted Scorecard Area  | What It Signals      | Investor's Question |
|---|---|---|
| Financial forecasts          | Business may not have a financial model | "Can you share your 3-year financial projections?" |
| Valuation defence            | Founder cannot articulate a valuation methodology | "How did you arrive at your current valuation? Walk me through the methodology." |
| Transaction advisor          | No advisor engaged — may slow the deal process | "Are you working with a transaction advisor? If not, are you open to engaging one?" |
| Targeted investor list       | Founder has not done investor research | "Who else are you in conversation with and what types of investors are you targeting?" |
| Capital raising process knowledge | First-time fundraiser — may need more hand-holding | "Have you raised capital before? What does your timeline look like for this round?" |
| Marketing materials          | Incomplete investor document pack | "What materials do you have prepared — pitch deck, teaser, financial model?" |
| Alternative financing explored | Founder is dependent on a single capital path | "What other financing options have you explored alongside this equity raise?" |
