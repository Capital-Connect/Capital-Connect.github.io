# Financials

> Track and manage your business financial statements to support investor readiness and decision making.
> Financials are organized into three statements: Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow.
> Access Financials at [app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials)

> Three statements: Income Statement | Balance Sheet | Cash Flow
## What This Section Is
 
The Financials section is where founders enter their business financial statements directly into the platform. These figures feed into the Business Valuation report, support investor due diligence, and provide the financial foundation for the Lara AI readiness analysis. They are not a document upload — they are structured data entry into the platform's financial model.
 
**Why this matters:** Investors and You (Lara AI) analysis read these figures. A complete, accurate set of financials:
- Strengthens the Eligibility and Preparedness scores
- Feeds directly into any Business Valuation report requested
- Allows (You) Lara AI to give financially grounded readiness feedback
- Gives investors a structured view of the business before they even open the pitch deck

**Your framing before starting:** *"The Financials section is where you enter your actual financial statements — Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow. These feed directly into your valuation report and investor readiness scores. Have your latest financial statements in front of you before we begin — do not enter figures from memory."*

## Income Statement
 
> URL: https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials/income-statement
> Shows financial performance over a period of time.
 
### Fields — What You Know
 
| Field                     | What It Is                   | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue**               | Total income generated | Top line — all income before any deductions |
| **Advisory Services**     | Revenue from advisory services specifically | Enter if applicable; leave blank if not a revenue stream |
| **Cost of Sales**         | Direct costs of producing goods or services | COGS — raw materials, direct labour, direct overheads |
| **Gross Profit**          | Revenue minus Cost of Sales | Auto-calculated if platform computes it; otherwise: Revenue − Cost of Sales |
| **Gross Margin**          | Gross Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 | Should be consistent with Business Factsheet entry |
| **Operating Expenses**    | Total costs of running the business | All indirect costs — rent, utilities, marketing, admin |
| **Staff Costs**           | Salaries and related employee expenses | Separate from Cost of Sales if staff costs are indirect |
| **EBITDA**                | Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortisation | Gross Profit minus Operating Expenses (including Staff Costs) |
| **EBITDA Margin**         | EBITDA ÷ Revenue × 100 | Should match Business Factsheet entry — cross-check for consistency |
| **Depreciation & Amortisation** | Non-cash reduction in asset value | Add back to EBITDA when calculating operating cash flow |
| **EBIT**                  | Earnings Before Interest and Tax | EBITDA minus D&A |
| **Interests**             | Interest expenses on outstanding debt | Higher if business is leveraged |
| **Profit Before Tax**     | EBIT minus interest | Pre-tax earnings |
| **Taxes**                 | Tax obligations for the period | Apply correct tax rate for the jurisdiction |
| **Net Profit**            | Final profit after all deductions | Bottom line — should match Business Factsheet net profit entry |
 
**Red figures = negative values (losses or deficits).** This is expected behaviour — not an error. You normalise this for the founder: *"Red figures indicate negative values — a loss or deficit in that line. This is normal for early-stage businesses and expected by investors. What matters is the trend and the path to profitability."*

## Income Statement
> An income statement is a financial statement that shows the financial performance of a business over a period of time. It is used to track the financial performance of a business and to make decisions about the business.
> Access at [app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials/income-statement](https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials/income-statement)

### Fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | Total income generated by the business |
| **Advisory Services** | Revenue specifically from advisory services rendered |
| **Cost of Sales** | Direct costs attributable to the production of goods or services sold |
| **Gross Profit** | Revenue minus Cost of Sales |
| **Gross Margin** | Gross Profit as a percentage of Revenue |
| **Operating Expenses** | Total costs incurred in running the business |
| **Staff Costs** | Employee salaries and related expenses |
| **EBITDA** | Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, and Amortisation |
| **EBITDA Margin** | EBITDA as a percentage of Revenue |
| **Depreciation & Amortisation** | Reduction in value of assets over time |
| **EBIT** | Earnings Before Interest and Tax |
| **Interests** | Interest expenses on debt |
| **Profit Before Tax** | Earnings before tax deductions |
| **Taxes** | Tax obligations for the period |
| **Net Profit** | Final profit after all deductions including tax |

> Figures shown in red indicate negative values (losses or deficits).

| Field                             | Description |

### Internal Consistency Checks — You Run These
 
Revenue − Cost of Sales = Gross Profit ✓
Gross Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 = Gross Margin ✓
Gross Profit − Operating Expenses − Staff Costs = EBITDA (approximately) ✓
EBITDA − D&A = EBIT ✓
EBIT − Interests = Profit Before Tax ✓
Profit Before Tax − Taxes = Net Profit ✓
EBITDA Margin = EBITDA ÷ Revenue × 100 — must match Business Factsheet ✓
Net Profit — must match Business Factsheet ✓
 
If any check fails: *"The figures in [line] don't appear to be internally consistent — [specific issue]. Let's check the source data before saving, because investors and the valuation engine will flag this."*

## Balance Sheet
 
> URL: https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials/balance-sheet
> Shows financial position at a specific point in time.
 
### Fields — What You Know
 
**Assets**
 
| Field                        | What It Is | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Land & Property**          | Book value of land and property | Use book value from latest audited or management accounts |
| **Plant & Equipment**        | Machinery, tools, equipment — net of depreciation | Net book value after accumulated depreciation |
| **Other Non-Current Assets** | Long-term assets not elsewhere classified | Intangibles, long-term investments, deposits |
| **Trade Receivables**        | Amounts owed by customers | Outstanding invoices — not cash yet received |
| **Cash**                     | Cash and cash equivalents | Bank balances and short-term liquid instruments |
| **Inventory**                | Goods held for sale | At cost or net realisable value |
| **Other Current Assets**     | Short-term assets not elsewhere classified | Prepayments, VAT receivable, etc. |
| **Total Assets**             | Sum of all assets | Should equal Total Liabilities + Shareholders Equity |
 
**Liabilities**
 
| Field                         | What It Is            | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Trade Payables**            | Amounts owed to suppliers | Outstanding invoices not yet paid |
| **Other Current Liabilities** | Short-term obligations not elsewhere classified | Accruals, deferred revenue, tax payable |
| **Loans**                     | Outstanding loan balances | All debt — bank loans, director loans, convertible notes |
| **Other Non-Current Liabilities** | Long-term obligations not elsewhere classified | Deferred tax, long-term provisions |
| **Total Liabilities**         | Sum of all liabilities | |
 
**Equity**
 
| Field                   | What It Is | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital**             | Funds invested by owners or shareholders | Paid-up share capital |
| **Other Reserves**      | Retained reserves not elsewhere classified | Share premium, revaluation reserves |
| **Retained Earnings**   | Cumulative profits retained | Carried forward from prior years — connects to income statement |
| **Dividends**           | Distributions paid to shareholders | Reduces retained earnings |
| **Shareholders Equity** | Total equity attributable to shareholders | Capital + Reserves + Retained Earnings − Dividends |
 
### Internal Consistency Check — The Accounting Equation
Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Shareholders Equity
 
**This must balance.** If it does not, there is a data entry error. You flag this immediately: *"The balance sheet doesn't balance — Total Assets don't equal Total Liabilities plus Shareholders Equity. This means there's a data entry error somewhere. Let's find it before moving on."*
 
**Additional cross-checks:**
Retained Earnings (current year) = Retained Earnings (prior year) + Net Profit − Dividends ✓
Loans on balance sheet — should match total debt entered in Business Factsheet ✓
Cash on balance sheet — should match Ending Cash on Cash Flow statement ✓

## Cash Flow Statement
 
> URL: https://app.capitalconnect.africa/business/financials/cash-flows
> Shows movement of cash in and out of the business.
 
### Fields — What You Know
 
**Operating Activities**
 
| Field                           | What It Is                   | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit Before Tax**           | Starting point for cash flow | Should match Profit Before Tax from Income Statement |
| **Depreciation & Amortisation** | Non-cash expense added back  | Positive adjustment — D&A reduces profit but doesn't use cash |
| **Taxes Paid**                  | Actual cash tax payments      | May differ from tax charge on income statement (timing differences) |
| **Operating Cash Flow**         | Net cash from core operations | Before working capital movements |
 
**Working Capital Changes**
 
| Field                          | What It Is                       | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Change in Receivables**      | Increase = cash use; Decrease = cash source | If receivables grow, cash is tied up |
| **Change in Payables**         | Increase = cash source; Decrease = cash use | If payables grow, cash is conserved |
| **Change in Inventory**        | Increase = cash use; Decrease = cash source | Buying more stock uses cash |
| **Change in Other Current Assets** | Movement in other short-term assets | |
| **Change in Other Current Liabilities** | Movement in other short-term liabilities | |
| **Other Non-Current Assets**   | Movement in long-term assets | |
| **Other Non-Current Liabilities** | Movement in long-term liabilities | |
| **Net Cash from Operations**   | Total cash from operating + working capital | Key metric investors use to assess cash generation |
 
**Investing Activities**
 
| Field                           | What It Is                   | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Property, Plant & Equipment** | Capex spend or proceeds from asset disposals | Negative = spending on assets; Positive = selling assets |
| **Change in Land & Property**   | Cash related to land and property | |
| **Net Cash from Investing**     | Total cash used in or generated from investing | High capex reduces free cash flow |
 
**Financing Activities**
 
| Field                      | What It Is | Your Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Movement in Borrowings** | Cash from new loans or debt repayments | Positive = new borrowing; Negative = repayment |
| **Change in Equity**       | Cash from new equity raised or returned | Positive = new equity raised |
| **Dividends Paid**         | Cash distributed to shareholders | Should match Dividends on Balance Sheet |
| **Net Cash from Financing** | Total cash from financing activities | 
 
**Summary**
 
| Field | What It Is | Lara's Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Net Cash Flow** | Total net movement in cash | Net Cash from Operations + Investing + Financing |
| **Opening Cash** | Cash at start of period | Should match prior year Ending Cash |
| **Ending Cash** | Cash at end of period | Should match Cash on Balance Sheet |
 
### Internal Consistency Check
 
Profit Before Tax (Cash Flow) = Profit Before Tax (Income Statement) ✓
Net Cash Flow = Net Cash from Operations + Net Cash from Investing + Net Cash from Financing ✓
Ending Cash = Opening Cash + Net Cash Flow ✓
Ending Cash (Cash Flow) = Cash (Balance Sheet) ✓

 
**You flag the critical linkage:** *"Ending Cash on the Cash Flow statement must equal the Cash line on the Balance Sheet. If they don't match, there's an inconsistency in the data — investors will catch this in due diligence."*

## Cross-Statement Consistency — The Three-Statement Link
 
You check these connections across all three statements after entry:
 
| Connection         | What to Check |
|---|---|
| Income Statement → Balance Sheet | Net Profit feeds into Retained Earnings (via equity section) |
| Income Statement → Cash Flow | Profit Before Tax is the starting point for operating cash flow |
| Balance Sheet → Cash Flow | Ending Cash on Cash Flow = Cash on Balance Sheet |
| Balance Sheet → Cash Flow | Changes in working capital items must reconcile |
| Balance Sheet → Business Factsheet | Total debt (Loans) = Debt entered in Factsheet Step 2 |
| Income Statement → Business Factsheet | Net Profit = Net Profit entered in Factsheet Step 1 |
| Income Statement → Business Factsheet | EBITDA Margin = EBITDA Margin entered in Factsheet Step 1 |
 
**Your three-statement sign-off:** *"Before we leave the Financials section — the three statements need to be internally linked. Net Profit flows into Retained Earnings. Profit Before Tax is the starting point for the cash flow. And Ending Cash must match the Balance Sheet. Are all three entered and do they connect?"*
